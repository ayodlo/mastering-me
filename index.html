Hurt has or will try to invade our lives to test the validity of our human existence. Only the strong will survive the blows that hurt throws.

Mastering Me is a journey of self-discovery as the author uses the Bible as a mirror for each individual soul to look into. She tells her story of a young unlearned, unchurched and gullible married minister who uknowingly didn't expect to deal with controlling, hateful and unloving individuals in the church.

She characterizes the pain of loss, rejection, abondonment, and hopelessness and deals with the mental and emotional bouts that come along with it all. She cleverly uses biblical characters like Samson to demonstrate the loss of power and falling into the enemy's hand.

Through out Mastering Me, Author Tashuna Scott describes her battles with suicide, spans of depression, a loss of self-worth and self identification as she was bewitched into living a double life in the church.

Then she brings the reader to the pivotal point of change and describes the upward spiral of moving towards restoration and a new life.
